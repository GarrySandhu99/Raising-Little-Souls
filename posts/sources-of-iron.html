Sources of Iron

2026-02-16

Iron is an essential nutrient for growing babies. Below are common, safe sources to consider as you plan meals and feeds. Always speak with your pediatrician about timing and portion sizes for your child.

Top Iron Sources

  • Red meat: Beef and lamb provide highly bioavailable heme iron.
  • Poultry and fish: Good options for older infants as tolerated.
  • Legumes: Lentils, chickpeas, and beans are excellent plant-based sources.
  • Leafy greens: Spinach and cooked greens provide iron (pair with vitamin C-rich foods).
  • Fortified cereals: Many infant cereals are fortified with iron â€” useful for weaning.
  • Tofu and tempeh: Good plant-based alternatives.

Tips to Improve Iron Absorption

Pair iron-rich foods with vitamin C (citrus, berries, bell pepper) and avoid giving tea or calcium-rich drinks at the same meal, which can inhibit absorption.

When to Seek Advice

If you're concerned about growth, pallor, or feeding, speak with your pediatrician. They may check iron status and offer personalised guidance.
