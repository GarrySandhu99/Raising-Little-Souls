First Foods — When and What to Offer

2025-02-12

Starting solids is a big milestone. Here's a simple, no-fuss overview of when and what to offer.

When to start

Most babies are ready for solid foods around 6 months—when they can sit with support, hold their head steady, and show interest in food (reaching, opening their mouth). Your health visitor can help you decide if your baby is ready.

What to offer first

  • Single-ingredient foods are easiest: mashed banana, avocado, cooked sweet potato or carrot, or baby rice mixed with your baby's usual milk.
  • Texture: Start smooth and soft; as they get used to it, you can leave it a bit lumpier.
  • One new food at a time so you can spot any reactions.

Foods to avoid early on

  • Honey before 12 months (risk of infant botulism).
  • Whole nuts and hard chunks that could choke (nut butters are fine in small amounts).
  • Added salt and sugar — babies don't need them.

Go at your baby's pace

Some days they'll eat more, some days almost nothing. That's normal. Let them explore, get messy, and stop when they show they're done. You're building habits and curiosity, not finishing a plate.
