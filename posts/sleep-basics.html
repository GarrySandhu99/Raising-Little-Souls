Sleep

Baby Sleep Basics — Where to Start

2025-02-13

Sleep can feel like the biggest challenge in the first year. Here are a few basics that many families find helpful—no strict rules, just options to try.

Safe sleep matters

  • Place your baby on their back for every sleep.
  • Use a firm, flat surface with no loose bedding, bumpers, or toys.
  • Keep the room at a comfortable temperature and avoid overheating.

Day and night

Newborns don't know day from night yet. You can gently encourage the difference:

  • Day: More light, normal sounds, interaction.
  • Night: Dim lights, quiet, minimal interaction during feeds and changes.

Over time this helps their body clock adjust.

Routines (when you're ready)

A short, calm routine before bed can signal "sleep time." It doesn't have to be long—a feed, a nappy change, a song or a few minutes of cuddles, then into the crib. Same order, same kind of calm, can help.

Every baby is different

Some babies sleep in longer stretches early; others need more time. If you're worried about your baby's sleep or your own exhaustion, talking to your health visitor or GP is always a good step.
