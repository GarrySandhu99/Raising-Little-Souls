Bottle Feeding Your Baby: A Gentle Guide for Parents

2026-02-22

Whether you're formula feeding, supplementing, or offering expressed breast milk, bottle feeding is a tender moment of connection and nourishment. Here's a practical guide to help you feed your baby with confidence and care.

Getting the Basics Right

Bottle feeding isn't just about the bottle — it's about creating a calm, safe environment where your baby can eat comfortably. Hold your baby in a semi-upright position, support their head, and tilt the bottle so the nipple stays full of milk (not air). This helps reduce gas and makes feeding easier.

Paced Bottle Feeding

Paced feeding lets your baby control the flow and take breaks. Hold the bottle horizontally so milk doesn't pour in too quickly. Let your baby pause, breathe, and signal when they're full. This approach supports healthy eating habits and can help prevent overfeeding.

Choosing the Right Bottle and Nipple

Babies have different preferences. Some like wide nipples; others prefer a narrower fit. Start with a slow-flow nipple and move up only if your baby seems frustrated. If you're combining breast and bottle, a nipple that mimics the breast can help with the transition.

Safety Reminders

  • Never prop the bottle — always hold it and stay present.
  • Check that formula or milk is prepared and stored correctly.
  • Discard unfinished bottles within an hour of feeding.
  • Sterilize bottles and nipples before first use; wash thoroughly after each feed.

A Moment of Connection

Bottle feeding is more than nutrition — it's a chance to bond. Make eye contact, talk softly, and enjoy the quiet closeness. You're not just feeding your baby; you're letting them know they're safe and loved.
